About Me

The above picture is clearly not me, but the tagline "insanely curious" does apply. I like to learn and have many interests, which has led me to a very unique educational and career path. I started out pre-med on a national merit scholarship, but lost the scholarship the first year due to lack of discipline and no real grown-up skills. I then joined the navy nuclear program and operated nuclear reactors for 6 years from 1999-2005 similar to Homer Simpson, except on an aircraft carrier (USS Carl Vinson).

After the Navy, I started a construction company in New Orleans following Hurricane Katrina with a few friends, during which time I earned a Residential Homebuilder License. After about a year, New Orleans was mainly put back together and I wanted a more steady job with health benefits so I took a job at Samsung Austin Semiconductor as an equipment technician, where I currently still work. I chose night shift so I could go to college during the day and pursued a microbiology degree at the University of Texas on a path towards becoming a pharmacist/pharmaceutical researcher.

However, during my studies, I transitioned to an engineer role (despite my lack of a degree) and found I enjoyed that much more than microbiology. I thus completed my degree in microbiology only to then assume the official title of Engineer II at Samsung. 1 year later, I transferred to the IT / Systems department as a software architect and quickly transitioned into management. Having taken some online computer science classes at Harvard, I was accepted at Georgia Tech for a master's degree in computer science with a specialization in machine learning, where I am still enrolled.

I am now the manager of Software Architecture, UX / UI Design and Full Stack Development and have been in management for roughly 3 years. Although I love the intense problem solving that comes with working as an individual contributor, I feel that management, particularly in the field of computer science, is my true calling. Upon completion of this bootcamp, I will redouble my efforts at Georgia Tech and hope to graduate in 2022.
